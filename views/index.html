In search for a livable life the majority of migrants and refugees flowing into Europe take the Western Balkan route. The route stretches out through Greece, Macedonia, Serbia, and Croatia. Refugees would then continue their journey northward through Hungary, but after Hungary erected a barber-wire fence at the border with Serbia and Croatia in October the movement turned towards Slovenia. Since the end of October more than 250 000 migrants and refugees have passed through Slovenian borders. Here is the visualization of their movement through the country.
